User Oriented Collaborative Design

UOCD is a course that teaches how to talk to people and use design frameworks to create solutions that are driven by the needs, values and motivations of a user group. With team of 5 spent semester speaking with lawyers in legal aid and public defense eventually coming to understand the toll of second-hand trauma and the value of interoffice support. By the end of the semester had co-designed several ideas to support the values and tensions of this incredible group of people.

Newborn Warmer for Low Resource Hospitals

Otter is a newborn warmer for low resource hospitals that is being developed through a collaboration among three partners: Design That Matters (U.S. design firm), MTTS (a Vietnamese medical device company) and Affordable Design and Entrepreneurship (a class from Olin, Babson and Wellesley colleges which give students the opportunity to work on social ventures for developing areas). My work on Otter includes executing IEC compliance tests to evaluate heating element and prototyping to fix design issues. In Jan. 2018 I travelled to Vietnam to interface with manufacturer and codesign alarm system with healthcare workers and patient families.

Termite-Inspired Play Experience

With a team of five, created a play experience for 4th graders based on the tunneling and mound-building behaviors of termites. The final product was a 10 ft. x 14 ft. maze filled with nearly 100 building blocks.

Ramp Stage Environment

Co-designed a ramp stage environment for dancers Alice Sheppard and Laurel Lawson, who dance with wheelchairs. Sketch modelled and fabricated initial and final prototype; researched and tested ramp surfaces. Project with team of ten. Received coverage by the Boston Globe.

High-Altitude Ballooning Project

With funding from NASA and the Illinois Space Grant Consortium, participated in 2015 high-altitude ballooning project at the Chicago City Colleges. Conducted experiments looking at the effect of near-space radiation on chlorophyll levels in plants. Also set up Arduino gas sensors and data loggers and Raspberry Pi cameras to record data during flights.
